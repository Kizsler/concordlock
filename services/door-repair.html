What We Fix

When a Door Won't Behave

A door that won't latch, a lock that sticks, a closer that slams or won't shut — these aren't emergencies, but they're the kind of nagging problem that wears on a home, frustrates tenants, and leaves a business less secure than it should be. We fix the door, not just the lock.

Because we're a full locksmith shop, we carry the cylinders, strikes, closers, and hardware to solve most door problems in a single visit — residential and commercial, in Concord and throughout Contra Costa County.

01

Door Won't Latch or Close

Misaligned strikes, sagging doors, and worn latches are the most common call we get. We realign, adjust, and replace so the door catches and locks the way it should.

02

Patio & Sliding Door Locks

Sliding glass and patio doors use specialized locks and latches that hardware stores rarely stock. We repair and replace them — a real security upgrade for a commonly-overlooked entry point.

03

Door Closers

Commercial door closers that slam, drift, or won't pull the door shut — adjusted, rebuilt, or replaced with the correct size and rating for the door.

04

Hinges, Knobs & Levers

Squeaking, binding, or broken hinges; loose or failed knobs and lever sets. We replace worn door hardware with quality residential and commercial-grade parts.

05

Door Frames & Strikes

Kicked-in frames, stripped strike plates, and damaged jambs — reinforced and repaired so the door is solid and secure again.

06

Exit Devices & Panic Hardware

Panic bars, push-bars, and exit alarms (including Detex devices) installed, serviced, and kept code-compliant for commercial egress doors.

Hardware, Too

We Also Supply the Hardware

Need commercial door locks, closers, or exit hardware for a building? We source and install commercial-grade hardware — so you get parts rated for the job instead of whatever the big-box store had on the shelf.

Commercial-grade locks

Restricted keyways, pick-resistant cylinders, and BHMA-rated hardware built for heavy daily use.

Right part, right door

We match closers, hinges, and exit devices to the door's weight, fire rating, and traffic — not a one-size guess.

Repair or replace

We'll tell you honestly when a part is worth rebuilding and when replacement is the smarter spend.
